Practical 03 — Churn — Classification Metrics

In this week's practical we are going to take it easy (DON'T WORRY — I will have the first assignment published later in the week, to compensate for this) and will just focus on the impact of different classification metrics.

Our starting point is churn dataset that we used in Practical 02. This is a relatively clean dataset (no obvious issues with features - missing values, outliers, poor distributions, etc), and after one-hot encoding of categorical features the Logistic Regression model out perform multiple classifiers (including Decision Trees, Random Forest classifier, Ada Boost) and had a accuracy score (mean of CV=10) of 80.11%.

Also, recall that the dataset is somewhat imbalanced with 73.4%/2.6.6 split of No/Yes in the target column, so perhaps accuracy is not the best metric to use.

Task 2: business cost/benefit metric

Here we will consider "the real world" cost/benefit of a prediction model. Note that while my figures here are almost surly incorrect the general configuration is plausible.

The Telecom company wants to implement a rewards scheme to persuade customers, who otherwise would churn, to remain with the company. They are willing to operate under the following assumptions based on previous schemes.

  • The rewards scheme will be offered to all customers who are predicted to churn only. The cost of the reward scheme if taken up by a customer is 25 euro and incurs no cost if not taken up.

  • The average customer contributes 1,000 euro per year to the company if they do not churn.

  • In terms of the customers who are not intending to churn (Churn=False)

    • 100% of non-churn customers will continue to be customers, regardless of being offered the rewards scheme.
    • 60% of non-churn customers who are offered the rewards scheme will take up the offer.

  • In terms of the customers who are intending to churn (Churn=True)

    • 90% of churn customers who offered the rewards scheme take up the offer.
    • 100% of churn customers who do not take up the offer will churn.
    • 70% of churn customers who take up the offer will subsequently stay with the company for the next year.

Create your own metric to represent the above cost and generate a DataFrame listing the performance of each classifier on this metric.
